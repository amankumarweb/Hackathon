Corporate Law Overview

Corporate law, also known as business law or mercantile law, encompasses the body of laws that govern business and commercial transactions. It regulates corporate contracts, sales, consumer transactions, employment, and other aspects of business activities.

Contracts

Corporate law governs the formation, interpretation, and enforcement of contracts between businesses, including sales contracts, service agreements, leases, and employment contracts. It covers aspects such as offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity, legality, and enforceability.

Disputes

These arise when parties to a contract disagree about its terms, performance, or interpretation. Disputes may involve issues such as breach of contract, non-payment, failure to deliver goods or services as agreed, or disputes over contract termination.

Resources

Law School Libraries: Law school libraries often provide access to a wide range of commercial law resources, including textbooks, treatises, legal encyclopedias, law reviews, and other reference materials.
